“With each breath we draw, we are closer to dust. I am not afraid of becoming a star” -Milene Minssieux

About me: Milene is a Copenhagen-based poet who draws inspiration from her life as a woman in the contemporary world. Her writing encompasses the lyrical, bodily expressions, the fluidity of water and inner landscapes.

As a yoga teacher and an acupuncturist, she finds inspiration in these holistic views. Through her words, she incorporates movement, circularity, and the pulsations that move us.

She invokes values such as sensitivity or frailty, and collapsing as a way to persist. She initiates a spur for the feminine to breathe and unfold. To find strength in being a woman and unison in our experience.

Poem of the month:


Hope

He drew a line
A streak on a page
A parting in the sand
She drew a circle
The line was taken
Into the world
And the earth was broken
Into lands
His story
Became history
And her circle was lost
Some say they've seen
Rings in the sand
Or spheres hanging Over closed lids
I bet they're on to something
An exit
Throats widening
Like two dancers
Drawing circles
To a beating heart

-Milene
